# Sky Fury

Native Android landscape shooter set around WW2-era aircraft silhouettes and weapons.

## Build

This machine does not expose Java on the shell `PATH`, so the helper script uses Android Studio's bundled JDK when needed:

```sh
./build_debug.sh
```

Equivalent direct command:

```sh
JAVA_HOME="/Applications/Android Studio.app/Contents/jbr/Contents/Home" ./gradlew assembleDebug
```

The debug APK is written to:

```text
app/build/outputs/apk/debug/app-debug.apk
```

## Gameplay

- Landscape-only Android game.
- Upward play direction: the player's aircraft starts near the bottom and fires upward.
- Light blue sky background with slow intermittent drifting clouds.
- Boss fights shift the sky darker and gloomier, with occasional lightning flashes.
- Fixed joystick on the lower-left, with an optional touch-drag control style in settings.
- Stored special weapon button on the lower-right.
- Pause button during play, with resume, settings, restart, and menu actions.
- Waves scale enemy count, health, speed, fire cadence, and boss health on a steeper curve.
- Waves continue indefinitely, with bosses appearing after each wave's kill target.
- Enemy tiers:
  - Basic enemy: lowest HP, slow single-line fire.
  - General enemy: moderate HP, rapid zig-zag movement, moderate dual-line fire.
  - Boss enemy: highest HP, uses spread, spray, spiral, and later laser attacks.
- Each wave ends with a heavy bomber boss whose HP increases every wave.
- Defeated bosses trigger a short uplifting victory sting and a larger smoke-heavy explosion sequence.
- Boss weapons escalate by wave: spread and spray first, spiral bursts from wave 2, and telegraphed laser strikes from wave 4 onward.
- Player starts with 3 lives and loses a life only after HP is depleted.
- Player and all enemies display HP bars.
- Power-ups can drop after kills:
  - Heavy machine gun, permanent until the player loses a life.
  - Missile launcher, permanent until the player loses a life.
  - Immortality, 5 seconds.
  - Extra life.
  - Laser upgrade, stored as the only command-triggered special weapon and released for 1.5 seconds.
  - Gun barrage, permanent fan-shaped spread fire until the player loses a life.
  - Guardian angel, 5 seconds with two escort fighters.
- Player aircraft flashes briefly when hit.
- Mission failure shows score and celebrates new high scores with fanfare and confetti.

## Settings

- Plane type selector with preview:
  - Messerschmitt Me 262A.
  - Gloster Meteor F.3.
  - Supermarine Spitfire Mk V.
  - North American P-51D Mustang.
  - Bell XP-59A Airacomet.
  - de Havilland Vampire F.1.
  - McDonnell TD2D Katydid.
- Music and SFX volume sliders.
- Control style selector for fixed joystick or touch-drag movement.

## Assets And Accuracy

Visuals use the supplied PNG sprites in `app/src/main/res/drawable-nodpi`. Player aircraft and power-up icons use the supplied assets, basic enemies use the Grumman F8F Bearcat, general enemies use the Lockheed P-38 Lightning, and boss aircraft are randomly selected from B-24 Liberator, Avro Lancaster, B-29 Superfortress, B-25 Mitchell, and B-17 Flying Fortress. Canvas drawing remains as a fallback for missing sprites and for effects such as smoke, lasers, lightning, and explosions.

The bundled audio files are original procedural WAV assets generated by `tools/generate_audio.js`, so they are royalty-free for this project and do not require attribution.
